# SWOT Analysis — Reference

Inline reference for the `situation-analysis` skill. Not a standalone skill.

## Structure

| | Helpful | Harmful |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal** (within the org's control) | **Strengths** | **Weaknesses** |
| **External** (outside the org's control) | **Opportunities** | **Threats** |

## Quality rules

**Strengths:**
- Must be current capabilities or assets, not aspirations
- Must be defensible — "good team" is generic; "3 senior SEO strategists with 10+ year track records in multifamily" is defensible
- Ask: "Would a competitor agree this is a strength?"

**Weaknesses:**
- Must be internal — if it's external, it's a threat
- Must be honest — the SWOT fails if weaknesses are softened into "areas for improvement"
- Ask: "If a competitor knew this, would they exploit it?"

**Opportunities:**
- Must be external — if it's internal, it's a strength being underutilized (move it)
- Must be actionable within the stated timeframe
- Must be specific to the market/industry, not generic ("AI is changing everything" is not an opportunity statement)

**Threats:**
- Must be external — if it's internal, it's a weakness
- Must be plausible within the stated timeframe
- Include competitive threats, market shifts, regulatory changes, customer behavior changes, and macro factors
- "Competition" is not a threat. "RentCafe bundling PPC into their platform at lower margin" is a threat.

## Common mistakes

- Listing internal weaknesses as threats (or vice versa)
- Generic filler that applies to any company ("skilled workforce" as strength, "economic uncertainty" as threat)
- All strengths and no weaknesses (indicates the analysis wasn't honest)
- Opportunities and threats that aren't actionable
- No "so what" — a SWOT without a synthesis paragraph is an academic exercise

## Presentation format

Present each quadrant in its own message with 3–5 bullet points each. End with a synthesis message: "The SWOT suggests [strategic implication]. The key tension is [strength vs. threat or weakness vs. opportunity]."
