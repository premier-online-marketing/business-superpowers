# Porter's Five Forces — Reference

Inline reference for the `situation-analysis` skill. Not a standalone skill.

## The five forces

1. **Threat of new entrants** — How easy is it for new competitors to enter this market? Barriers: capital requirements, brand loyalty, regulatory, network effects, switching costs.

2. **Bargaining power of suppliers** — How much leverage do the people/platforms you depend on have? In a services business, "suppliers" often means talent, technology platforms, and data providers.

3. **Bargaining power of buyers** — How much leverage do your customers have? Factors: buyer concentration, switching costs, price sensitivity, availability of substitutes, information asymmetry.

4. **Threat of substitutes** — What else could your customer use instead of you? Substitutes aren't competitors — they're entirely different approaches to solving the same problem. For a marketing agency, a substitute might be an in-house team, a SaaS platform, or doing nothing.

5. **Competitive rivalry** — How intense is competition among existing players? Factors: number of competitors, industry growth rate, differentiation, exit barriers, fixed costs.

## Quality rules

- **Rate each force** as High / Medium / Low with a one-sentence justification
- **Be specific to the company and market** — "buyer power is high" is generic; "buyer power is high because PMCs with 500+ units can negotiate retainer discounts and have 3+ alternative agencies bidding" is grounded
- **Don't confuse forces.** The most common error is listing competitors under "threat of new entrants" (that's rivalry) or listing competitors under "substitutes" (substitutes are different categories entirely)
- **Include the "so what"** — which forces are most threatening to the company's position, and what does that imply for strategy?

## Presentation format

Present one force per message (5 messages). Each message includes: force name, High/Medium/Low rating, 2–3 evidence bullets, and one-sentence strategic implication. End with a synthesis: "The overall industry attractiveness is [assessment]. The dominant forces are [X and Y], which means [strategic implication]."
